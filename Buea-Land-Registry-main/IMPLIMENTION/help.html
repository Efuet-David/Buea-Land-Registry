Frequently Asked Questions
  • How do I register my land on BueaLand Registry?
    Navigate to Register Land from the sidebar. Fill in the 5-step form with your plot details, owner information, GPS coordinates, document uploads, and confirmation. Have your National ID, surveyor's report, and proof of ownership ready.
  • How long does land verification take?
    Standard verification takes 5–10 working days after all required documents are submitted. Express processing reduces this to 2–3 days at an additional fee. You will receive a notification once your plot status updates.
  • What documents are required for registration?
    You need: (1) National Identity Card, (2) Surveyor's / Cadastral Plan, (3) Deed of Sale or Title Deed, (4) Municipal Development Plan, (5) Two passport photographs. All documents must be within 6 months.
  • How do I transfer land ownership?
    From My Properties, select the plot and click Transfer Ownership. Enter the recipient's account ID or NIC. Both parties must confirm, and a transfer fee of 25,000 FCFA applies.
  • Can I update my land information after registration?
    Yes. Minor updates can be requested via Amendment Request. Major changes like plot subdivisions require a new surveyor's plan and additional fees.
  • What does a "Disputed" status mean?
    A third party has raised a claim against your plot. Contact our support team immediately or visit the Buea Land Registry office. Submit additional legal documentation to resolve the dispute.
